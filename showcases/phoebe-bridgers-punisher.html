Punisher by Phoebe Bridgers

Album Showcase

Are you ready to cry? Punisher is the second album from California artist Phoebe Bridgers, continuing on from her 2017 debut, Stranger in the Alps. Her music is beautiful blend of indie pop with a folk ideas mixed in. Between solo albums, she collaborated with Conor Oberst on the indie rock project Better Oblivion Community Center, and before that on the indie folk rock collaboration project boygenius with Julien Baker and Lucy Dacus.

On this release Phoebe solidifies her style, creating a serious of multi-dimentional songs that together create something very powerful. In the editor notes on Apple Music, the writer talks about the album being "like a diary about your crush during the apocalypse" - I think that sums the album up almost perfectly.

Garden Song was the first single released for the album, with lyrics that blend nightmares and fantasies together, And when your skinhead neighbor goes missing \ I'll plant a garden in the yard. At first I thought the male voice was Matt Berninger, with his low and monotone voice (spoiler: it isn't). The song Kyoto follows comes next on the album, and is accompanied by one of the coolest music videos I've ever seen. It's also the most upbeat track on the album, unlike Garden Song which mixed nightmares with fantasies, Kyoto is just fantasies.

The ending of the album is just as strong as the start, with the songs Graceland Too and I Know The End. Graceland Too starts with a fiddle and a banjo, unfamiliar instruments for a Phoebe Bridgers song and the song is about a woman re-entering a place that feels unfimiliar - full of possibility. She can do anything she wants to. The album ends with a two parter, I Know The End, in which she sings about the anxiety of leaving home - comparing it to an apocalypse. The song edges up towards a chaotic ending, when anxiety finally takes over. The end is near are the words that are repeated leading up to the end, where there is a scream - Phoebe letting her emotions out.

I love this album, maybe more so than her debut - give it a listen.

June 2020
