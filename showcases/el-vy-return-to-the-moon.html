Return to the Moon by EL VY (Matt Berninger and Brent Knopf)

Album Showcase

Return of the Moon is the 2015 collaboration project between The National frontman Matt Berninger and multi-instrumentalist and producer Brent Knopf. The album sees Matt separated from his usual surroundings of melancholic indie rock with this album seeing Matt take a more fruitful and upbeat step incorporating some art rock ideas. This was something I was sceptical off at first, due to my love for The National.

The album kicks off with the title track, Return to the Moon (Political Song for Didi Bloome to Sing, with Cresendo), which like the name suggests, is a political song with some incredibly catchy lyrics. Glimpses of The National definitely appear throughout the album with songs like Paul is Alive and No Time to Crank the Sun, but often with a funkier sound behind the lyrics.

It's a Game is something straight out of a Phoebe Bridgers album, but with male vocals. The song starts with an atmospheric sound which can only be described as "Phoebe Bridgers". Phoebe supported The National on their I Am Easy to Find tour in 2017, and Matt and Phoebe have released music together since then. My favourite track on the album is definitely Happiness, Missouri, which transitions from the previous song Sad Case and is the loudest part of the album. The song appeared in my Discover Weekly and is how I finally ended up listening to the album, and I'm definitely happy I did.

May 2020
