Jump Rope Gazers by The Beths

Album Showcase

Jump Rope Gazers is the second album from New Zealand indie rockers The Beths and follows on from their raw and energetic debut Future Me Hates Me. I first saw these guys when they supported Death Cab for Cutie back in early 2019 and then again as headliners a few months later. Their debut sound made for a perfect small-venue gig experience, and although this newest project still has those high-flying tracks - they're partnered with slower, more thought-provoking ballads which makes for a different listening experience than we're used to.

Building up to the release of this record, the band were doing at home live shows due to the virus. The final of which was a release party for the album, which was great fun to watch. Having listened to the album many many many times since release, I have to admit I love every song. So narrowing it down to only a couple to showcase was quite hard.

The first single that was released ahead of this record, Dying to Believe, is still definitely one of my favourites. The guitar riff that carries us into the song is so good, definitely something that I'll be looking up a tab for soon. Another highlight of the album is definitely Out of Sight, and the beautiful guitar riffs make a return here too. There's a fantastic live studio performance of this song available from RNZ Music where you can see the guitars in action. The Aaah's in the middle of this song make it really special.

There's also an acoustic song on the record, in You Are a Beam of Light - which is not something that I expected to hear. The harmonies on this track are elevated by the stripped back acoustic guitar and make for a mesmerising listen. Throughout the album you can definitely hear Death Cab's influence, maybe the acoustic track here was inspired by Death Cab's I Will Follow You into the Dark. Although this album is definitely different to their debut, it's still just as good and I cannot wait to see live again in April, and maybe Liz will whip out the acoustic guitar for a sing-along.

June 2020
