Album Showcase

The Sunset Tree by The Mountain Goats

The American indie folk rock band The Mountain Goats' 2005 project The Sunset Tree is this weeks album of choice. Like most of John Darnielle's work, his vocals is largly the main focus of the music. With his poetic lyrics he explores his teenage years, mostly revolving around his abusive stepfather.

Although I'm most familiar with their newer projects, Goths and my personal favourite In League with Dragons, it was this live performance on The Late Show with Stephen Colbert that drew me to The Sunset Tree.

In it, Darnielle tells a story about himself at seventeen "breaking free" from his childhood home to go for a drive to meet a girl and play video games. However, it was the lyrics I am going to make it though this year \ If it kills me that really caught my attention. It made me think about the situation we're all currently facing, and how the only think we can do at the moment is wait for it to be over so that we can continue with our lives. Of couse, that's not what he's singing about - not in 2005 anyway.

Other highlights of the album include the progressive guitar chords on the song Up the Wolves, a song that I often find myself humming along to. The song Lion's Teeth has some cool moments too, with the stringed instruments scaling behind the lyrics And hold on \ For dear life, I hold on.

Overall, this album is perfect for when you just want to relax to some acoustic guitar with well-crafted lyricism, something that Darnielle never seems to fail at producing. But mainly, I enjoy this album because I can picture Stephen Colbert having the time of his life on stage singing alongside the band.

May 2020
