Sideways to New Italy by Rolling Blackouts Coastal Fever

Album Showcase

Rolling Blackouts Coastal Fever is a band formed in 2013 in Melbourne, Australia that is described as something between "tough pop" and "soft punk". Sideways to New Italy was the first time I had heard the band yet somehow it was put on my Spotify front page when it was released. The first thing I noticed was how upbeat the album was, carried through the super-intense and super-catchy guitars. I later learned that was because they play with three guitars and no bass, which allows them to layer different effects and riffs together.

The album itself rolls together nicely, without any sort of standout track. I enjoy all of the tracks on the record, but my favourite is probably Cameo with its burst of energy as it hits the first chorus that continues throughout the rest of the song - You take a high wire jump \ You feel time dripping away. The acoustic guitars on the next song, Sunglasses at the Wedding, sound colourful and peaceful. The lyrics Why are we always the last ones to leave? really complete the song (listen to it, you'll see why).

I don't really have much else to say about this record, I like it. It makes me happy.

June 2020
