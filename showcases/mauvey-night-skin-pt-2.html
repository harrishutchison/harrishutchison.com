Night Skin, Pt. 2 by Mauvey

EP Showcase

Mauvey is back, and this time with Pt. 2 of the Night Skin EP. Between Pt. 1 and Pt. 2, he also released YEYEYEYEYE featuring the songs YEYEYEYEYE and COLOURWAY - two songs which I love. Mauvey also just signed with W Songs (part of Warner Music UK), which is HUGE!

Pt. 2 includes two songs, Honest and Black Pope. The opener has some mesmerising instrumentation, and the lyric What a miracle it would be \ If we could reach what we both could see my dear is really special. There's also a music video for this song, with the full lyrics in the description.

Black Pope is a song about the struggles of growing up as a black person, and things that he would change if he had the super power that the Pope possesses. The lines I wanted to be white so bad, I would pray and I used to want another name, so they would stop the things they say really stand out to me as something really powerful, yet unbelievably upsetting. No one should be wishing that they were something else. I isolated that segment of the song and played it on repeat multiple times - just processing the words. I also really love the guitar solo that runs the song out.

Upon my first listen to this release I liked Honest over Black Pope, but after multiple streams Black Pope has become maybe my favourite Mauvey song. Everything about it is amazing, especially that guitar solo (somebody tab it for me please). The Night Skin merch is still on sale, and now there's some new designs. All the profit is going towards The Night Skin Initiative, which is a fund awarding black creative artists the financial support they need to consider making their creativity a viable full-time career.

July 2020
